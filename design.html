DESIGN FOR MEDIA I & II PROJECTS

DESIGN I FOR MEDIA

The final template of my Shape and Pattern Project for my class, Design I for Media. In this project, I explored the tension of equivocal balance between black + white and positive + negative shapes. I also explored ideas of balance through symmetry and asymmetry and used complex shapes to create a pattern with an ambiguous figure-ground relationship. The project had three components: a hand-cut paper Notan, digital patterns with crops derived from my Notan, and a final concept design - making a digital composite of my pattern into a proposed space. All digital work was completed in Adobe Photoshop.

The final template of my Value Translation Project for my class, Design I for Media. I created a self-portrait that is inspired by a film-still of a portrait that I sourced from an assigned movie. Once I chose my inspiration and used my own means - a camera, lighting, props, and mimicry, I did the best I could to recreate the pose, value, lighting, and expression in your selection. Next, I digitally broke down the value pattern in black, white, and shades of gray in my image to fill into a grid. I digitally added a color-coded system based on colors+ numbers that fills in the grid based on translating the values of my image into a coded map. This Map assisted me in identifying the value pattern. Next, I created a value scale through adjusting images of different textures, along with nine different sketches that represented the photographed textures. Finally, I used the posterized portrait and inserted my textures and sketches to create a portrait. All digital work was completed in Adobe Photoshop.
